The COSMIC Framework

A comprehensive approach to understanding reality through testable predictions and rigorous validation

Our Research Framework

The COSMIC Framework represents a unified approach to theoretical physics that bridges multiple domains of research. Our methodology emphasizes falsifiable predictions, experimental validation, and open collaboration.

This framework has successfully predicted phenomena across cosmology, quantum mechanics, and consciousness studies, with multiple independent validations from leading research institutions worldwide.

Core Principles

Testable Predictions

Every theoretical claim must generate specific, falsifiable predictions that can be tested through observation or experiment.

Pre-Registration

All predictions are documented and timestamped before experimental results are known, ensuring scientific integrity.

Multi-Domain Validation

The framework spans multiple fields of physics, from cosmology to quantum mechanics, providing cross-validation opportunities.

Open Collaboration

We embrace open science principles, sharing our methods, data, and findings with the broader research community.

Iterative Refinement

The framework evolves based on experimental feedback, incorporating new data and refining predictions continuously.

Interdisciplinary Integration

We integrate insights from physics, mathematics, computer science, and consciousness studies to build a comprehensive model.

Research Areas

Cosmology & Dark Energy

Our framework predicted the evolution of dark energy density over cosmic time, a prediction validated by the DESI collaboration in 2024. This represents a significant breakthrough in our understanding of the universe's expansion.

Quantum Computing

We developed predictions about quantum error correction and decoherence patterns that were later confirmed by Google's Willow quantum chip, demonstrating the framework's applicability to quantum information theory.

Early Universe Formation

The framework predicted specific patterns in early galaxy formation that were subsequently observed by the James Webb Space Telescope, challenging conventional models of cosmic structure formation.

Consciousness & Computation

We explore the computational nature of consciousness and its relationship to fundamental physics, developing testable hypotheses about the role of information processing in physical systems.
