About Ic² Institute

Advancing the understanding of reality through the lens of information, consciousness, and computation. We bridge theoretical physics, mathematics, and philosophy to explore the fundamental nature of existence.

Our Mission

The Ic² Institute (Information-Consciousness-Computation Institute) is dedicated to developing and validating comprehensive theoretical frameworks that explain the relationship between information, consciousness, and the computational substrate of reality.

We believe that understanding consciousness and reality requires integrating insights from multiple disciplines: theoretical physics, mathematics, computer science, neuroscience, and philosophy. Our work focuses on creating testable predictions and falsifiable hypotheses that can advance scientific understanding.

Theoretical Development

Building rigorous mathematical frameworks that unify quantum mechanics, relativity, and consciousness within a computational paradigm.

Experimental Validation

Designing testable predictions and collaborating with research institutions to validate theoretical claims through empirical investigation.

Knowledge Dissemination

Making complex theoretical frameworks accessible through publications, educational content, and community engagement.

The Author

Michael K. Baines

Michael K. Baines is a theoretical researcher focused on understanding the fundamental nature of reality through information theory, consciousness studies, and computational models. His work synthesizes insights from physics, mathematics, and philosophy to develop comprehensive frameworks that address longstanding questions about existence, awareness, and the structure of the universe.

His primary work, The Big TOE: A Theory of Everything, presents a unified framework integrating quantum mechanics, relativity, and consciousness within a computational substrate. The theory makes specific testable predictions about quantum decoherence, consciousness signatures, and computational limits.

Michael approaches theoretical work with a commitment to scientific rigor, falsifiability, and open science principles. All predictions are documented with publication dates to establish clear timelines for validation, and experimental methodologies are made publicly available to enable independent verification.
