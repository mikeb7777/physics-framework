Testing Schedule

Upcoming experiments and observations that will test framework predictions

How We Test Predictions

Every prediction made by the COSMIC Framework is documented with timestamps before experimental results become available. This page tracks upcoming experiments and observations that will test our theoretical predictions. We maintain complete transparency—documenting both successes and failures.

2025-2026

Euclid Mission Galaxy Surveys

The Euclid space telescope will conduct extensive surveys of galaxy distributions and large-scale structure, providing additional tests of cosmic asymmetry predictions.

Framework Prediction:

Euclid will detect systematic directional variations in:

  • Galaxy clustering patterns aligned with cosmic axis
  • Large-scale structure asymmetries
  • Weak lensing signal variations by direction
Ongoing Mission
2025-2027

JWST Continued Early Galaxy Observations

James Webb Space Telescope continues deep field observations, expected to find even earlier galaxies.

Framework Prediction:

JWST will continue to find massive galaxies at increasingly high redshifts:

  • Galaxies at z > 15 (earlier than 270 million years)
  • Fully formed structures earlier than ΛCDM predicts
  • Systematic pattern continuing indefinitely
Ongoing Observations
2026-2027

Simons Observatory CMB Measurements

Next-generation cosmic microwave background observations with improved precision on hemispherical asymmetries.

Framework Prediction:

Enhanced measurements will confirm and refine detection of:

  • Hemispherical power asymmetry in CMB
  • Alignment with galaxy asymmetry axis
  • Increased statistical significance (> 4σ)
Upcoming
2026-2028

Quantum Computing Scaling Studies

Multiple teams working on scaling quantum error correction to larger logical qubit arrays.

Framework Prediction:

Information-theoretic principles predict continued scaling:

  • Exponential error reduction continues with array size
  • Below-threshold performance maintained
  • Information conservation limits confirmed
Multiple Projects
2027-2028

Vera C. Rubin Observatory First Light

The Rubin Observatory will conduct the Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST), mapping the southern sky.

Framework Prediction:

LSST will provide unprecedented statistical power to detect:

  • Large-scale asymmetries in galaxy distributions
  • Systematic variations in cosmic structure by direction
  • Correlation with other asymmetry measurements
Planned
2028-2030

Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope

Roman will provide wide-field infrared surveys complementing JWST's deep field observations.

Framework Prediction:

Roman's wide field will enable:

  • Statistical studies of early galaxy populations
  • Dark energy evolution measurements at multiple epochs
  • Large-scale structure mapping for asymmetry tests
Launch ~2027
2029+

Extremely Large Telescope (ELT)

Ground-based optical/infrared observations with unprecedented resolution and sensitivity.

Framework Prediction:

ELT's capabilities will enable:

  • Detailed spectroscopy of high-redshift galaxies
  • Confirmation of early galaxy structure and composition
  • Tests of cosmic acceleration evolution
First Light 2028
2030+

LiteBIRD CMB Polarization Mission

Space-based mission dedicated to mapping CMB polarization with extreme precision.

Framework Prediction:

Precision polarization measurements will reveal:

  • Enhanced detection of hemispherical asymmetries
  • Directional variations in primordial power spectrum
  • Potential detection of parity violations
Launch ~2032
