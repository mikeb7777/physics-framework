What This Framework Proposes

Physics has three pillars: quantum mechanics, relativity, and thermodynamics. This framework asks whether a fourth principle operates underneath them all: information processing.

We know from experiments that information is physical (Landauer's principle). We know quantum mechanics requires information conservation. We know the universe processes vast amounts of information at every scale.

The central question: If information processing is truly fundamental, can it explain patterns we currently treat as mysteries? Things like:

  • Why physical constants have the specific values we observe (fine-tuning)
  • Why brain networks and cosmic structure show identical statistical patterns
  • Whether gravity emerges from information patterns rather than being fundamental
  • How consciousness relates to physical information processing

This framework makes specific, testable predictions. It can be proven wrong. That's what makes it science rather than philosophy.
